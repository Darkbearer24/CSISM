Frequently Asked Questions

What is NCISM and why is it important? +
NCISM (National Commission for Indian System of Medicine) is the apex regulatory body in India that oversees the education, practice, and regulation of Indian Systems of Medicine including Ayurveda, Unani, Siddha, and Homeopathy. It establishes standards for colleges, curricula, and professional conduct.
How long does ISM college establishment typically take? +
The timeline varies based on institutional readiness, infrastructure development, and regulatory approvals. With proper planning and our guidance, most institutions can expect a 18-24 month process from initial planning to provisional affiliation.
What are the minimum infrastructure requirements? +
NCISM mandates specific requirements including classroom spaces, laboratory facilities, library resources, clinical departments, hostel facilities, and administrative offices. The exact specifications depend on the program level and system of medicine offered.
What qualifications are required for faculty members? +
Faculty must hold recognized degrees in their respective fields of ISM, relevant postgraduate qualifications, and professional experience. NCISM maintains a detailed teacher code system that specifies minimum qualifications for each position.
How does your consultancy ensure compliance? +
We conduct comprehensive compliance audits, document verification, infrastructure assessment, and faculty qualification checks. Our systematic approach ensures your institution meets all NCISM standards before formal submissions.
Is this a government website or private consultancy? +
CSISM is a private consultancy led by professionals with extensive expertise in ISM regulatory frameworks. We are independent consultants, not affiliated with NCISM or any government body. Our guidance is based on professional expertise and practical experience.
