Key Characteristics of IMCC Era (Siddha)

• Regulation driven by Minimum Standards (MSR/MSE)
• Strong focus on infrastructure and staffing numbers
• Limited inspection depth and enforcement
• No Teacher Code or individual faculty accountability
• No assessment or institutional rating framework
• Faculty issues largely treated as institutional, not personal

MSR → MESAR NCISM Era

What Changed for Siddha

• MSR/MSE → MESAR (Minimum Essential Standards, Assessment & Rating)
• Infrastructure-centric → functional + evidentiary compliance
• Checklist approach → clause-wise scrutiny
• No rating system → assessment & rating mandatory
• Weak penalties → strong statutory enforcement

Faculty Regulation NCISM Era

A Major Shift in Siddha

• Teacher Code mandatory; physical and functional verification
• Department-wise eligibility strictly examined
• “On-paper faculty” can attract penalties up to ₹25 lakh and one-year debarment
• NTET impacts eligibility, appointment, continuation, career progression

Compliance Impact NCISM Era

Practical Impact for Siddha Colleges

• Maintain continuous MESAR compliance
• Prepare for Technical Officer-level inspections
• Demonstrate integration of hospital, faculty, and academics
• Treat documentation as evidence, not formality
• Monitor faculty eligibility proactively (Teacher Code + NTET)
